Packaging Solutions

Process Timeline

Requirement Analysis

02
Concept Development
Solution concepts are developed that address protection requirements within cost and operational targets — and presented for customer evaluation.

MANUFACTURINGSOLUTIONS

CNC precision, lamination, fabrication and assembly — 27,000 sq.ft of in-house capability delivering consistent quality from prototype to production scale.

Overview

Manufacturing Solutions

Our manufacturing facility combines CNC precision, trained professionals, and high-volume production lines to deliver consistent quality at scale — from prototype runs to full production orders. 80+ trained professionals. 27,000 sq.ft under one roof. Quality inspection at every stage.

Our Process

Process Timeline

01
Requirement Analysis
Material specifications, volume requirements and performance standards are reviewed before production planning begins.
02
Concept Development
Manufacturing process routes are planned — which processes, in what sequence, with what tooling — to achieve the required output efficiently.
03
CNC Cutting
Precision CNC-controlled cutting for exact insert profiles, complex geometric cutouts and tight-tolerance components across all foam grades.
04
Lamination & Fabrication
Multi-layer adhesive lamination combines foam, film, board and fabric into integrated systems. Die cutting and thermoforming complete complex structural components.
05
Assembly
Multi-component packaging systems are assembled with precision alignment and consistency across every production batch.
06
Quality Control & Dispatch
Every batch undergoes dimensional and performance verification before dispatch. Consistent quality is the standard, not the exception.
Where We Apply It

Industries & Applications

Automotive Manufacturing
Where
High-volume foam packaging for component supply chains, PP crate systems for returnable logistics.
Why
Automotive supply chains demand packaging that performs identically across every production batch at high volume.
Solves
Inconsistent packaging quality, component damage from dimensional variation, and high single-use packaging costs.
